About Me

My journey into cybersecurity started with a strong interest in understanding how digital systems work and how to protect them. While studying at Al Akhawayn University, I joined The White Hats cybersecurity club, where I participated in CTF (Capture the Flag) challenges and beginner-level trainings that introduced me to key topics such as ethical hacking, cryptography, and system vulnerabilities.

I have gained valuable hands-on experience as an IT Support Specialist at Al Akhawayn University, where I've been working part-time for 1.5 years. In this role, I efficiently handle technical issues, troubleshoot problems, and maintain hardware for optimal performance. With strong communication skills, I engage effectively with students, faculty, and staff, ensuring prompt resolution of IT-related concerns.

Although I later decided to pause my studies and plan a transition to a new school starting in September, I've continued to pursue my passion independently. I'm currently working on the IBM Cybersecurity Analyst Certification and have completed 7 out of 14 courses, covering essential skills like threat intelligence, network defense, and incident response. I remain committed to building a career in cybersecurity and gaining both the technical and practical experience needed to succeed in the field.
